What to notice: in direct mode you steer using a picture of the past, and your corrections arrive in the future — the rover hits rocks you swerved to avoid. In supervised autonomy you send intent, not motion: the avatar plans its own path, reacts to hazards locally in seconds, and reports back when the goal is reached. That is the core idea of the Mars Avatar Project: control without presence.
